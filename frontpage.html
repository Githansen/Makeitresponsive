The people at Stanford university in USA, has invented a paper centrifuge that spins at up to 20 000 rpm (rotations per minute), and can isolate malaria parasites from whole human blood n 15 minutes. This paper centrifuge costs 20 cents, and require no electricity to work. It works by twisting and pulling the strings over and over, and this creates such high speeds that it will separate the parasites from the blood in 15 minutes or less. Normal centrifuges are massive, and costs several hundreds, possibly thousands of dollars.

The foldscope is a paper microscope that has a magnification of 140x and 2-micron resolution. With this microscope, you can see things lice bacteria, blood cells, single-celled organisms and even malaria parasites. And it only costs 50 cents to make!
